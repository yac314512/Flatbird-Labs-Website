For Designers & AI Image Tools
The Company
Flatbird Labs is a marketing transformation consultancy founded by three senior marketing leaders with Fortune 500 pedigree. They serve mid-market companies ($20M–$500M ARR) across industries, helping them access enterprise-grade marketing programs, AI-backed processes, and speed-to-market capabilities they couldn't build alone. The three founders are based in Philadelphia, Washington DC, and Amsterdam — with extended team presence in Austin and St. Louis.
Brand Personality
  • Confident without arrogance — experienced operators, not consultants
  • Warm and collegial — three friends, not a faceless firm
  • Modern and data-savvy — AI tools are part of the offering
  • Transatlantic in character — European design sensibility meets American energy
  • Playfully serious — "Flatbird" is whimsical; the work is rigorous
Logo Direction
  • A geometric, flat-style bird in flight — minimal polygons, no gradients
  • The bird should feel aerodynamic and purposeful, not cute or cartoonish
  • Optional: the bird form subtly suggests an upward arrow or forward momentum
  • Paired with wordmark: Syne ExtraBold — "Flatbird" in cream/dark, "Labs" slightly lighter or in accent
  • Works at small sizes (favicon, email signature) and large (signage, decks)
Visual Style
  • Flat geometric illustration — Low-poly, Bauhaus-influenced
  • Dark backgrounds preferred for hero/brand contexts
  • Rust red as the primary brand accent (not orange, not tomato)
  • Gold used sparingly as a premium highlight detail
  • Grid-based, structured layouts with generous negative space
  • Photography style: candid, real work environments — not stock smiles
Midjourney / DALL·E Prompt (Logo Concept)
"Flat geometric bird in flight, minimal polygon art, Bauhaus style, dark charcoal background, rust red and cream color palette, gold beak detail, bold angular wings, forward momentum, clean vector aesthetic, no gradients, no texture, brand logo mark, isolated on dark background, professional, modern"
What to Avoid
  • Gradients or drop shadows on the logo mark
  • Cute or cartoonish bird treatments
  • Purple or teal color schemes
  • Generic sans-serif wordmarks (Inter, Helvetica, Roboto)
  • Consulting clichés: handshakes, gear icons, upward bar charts
  • Overly complex mark that doesn't scale down
  • Literal AI imagery (robot heads, circuits as primary elements)
  • Anything that reads as "startup" rather than "seasoned firm"
